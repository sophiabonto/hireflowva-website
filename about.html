Chris Merz's Story

I'm Chris Merz — a licensed real estate agent, multifamily investor, podcast co-host, and the organizer of the Portland REI Meetup.

For a long time, I was doing all of it alone.

Client calls. Deal analysis. Meetup logistics. Podcast production. Marketing. Inbox. Spreadsheets. All of it.

I wasn't running out of ideas. I was running out of hours.

I knew I needed to delegate. I just didn't have the time to figure out how to hire, vet, and train someone — and actually trust them with my business.

So I built the process myself. Hired my first VA, refined the system, and watched what happened when the right person — properly trained — stepped into the work.

That VA became my partner. Today they run the systems that used to follow me into the evenings.

That experience is exactly what we built Hireflow VA to give other business owners and real estate professionals.

Our Approach

What we believe about hiring VAs

The principles that shape how we run every placement.

🎯

Fit beats availability

A VA who's available immediately but wrong for your business will cost you more than one worth waiting two weeks for. We don't rush fit.

🔧

Training is not optional

Smart people still need to know your systems. Untrained VAs fail not because they're incapable, but because they were never set up to succeed.

🤝

Placement is a relationship

We're not a job board. We stay involved after placement because a great hire at day one can still drift without proper support and check-ins.

📋

Process creates trust

Business owners struggle to delegate because they don't trust that things will be done right. SOPs and structured onboarding solve that problem directly.

💡

Simple pricing is a feature

Hidden fees and percentage-of-salary models create misaligned incentives. We charge a flat fee because that's what an honest service looks like.

🛡

Guarantees mean something

A guarantee that's hard to claim isn't a guarantee. Ours is simple: not the right fit in 30 days, we re-run the entire process at no cost. No exceptions.
